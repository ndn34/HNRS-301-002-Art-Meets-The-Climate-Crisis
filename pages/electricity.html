Electricity Consumption

Electricity often feels invisible. Lights turn on instantly, devices charge quietly, and climate control keeps spaces comfortable without much thought. Yet behind every kilowatt-hour is a network of power plants, transmission systems, and energy infrastructure that produces emissions and consumes resources. Everyday activities like charging phones, streaming videos, running computers, and cooling homes all contribute to electricity demand. The calculator below estimates how weekly habits translate into electricity use and carbon emissions, helping visualize how ordinary routines connect to the larger energy system.
